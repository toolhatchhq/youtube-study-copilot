# Unit Economics

## Pricing Options

- current launch configuration: `$19 lifetime`
- market subscription benchmark: roughly `$4.99/month`
- possible future hybrid: free + monthly subscription + lifetime option during launch windows

## Assumptions

- Chrome Web Store gives meaningful inbound traffic only if onboarding and listing quality are strong
- support burden must stay low because this is a solo-founder product
- browser-native AI and local storage keep variable cost low in the current version

## Cost Drivers

- Polar merchant-of-record transaction fees
- support time
- optional future cloud inference if browser-native AI proves insufficient
- analytics and monitoring tools once usage exceeds free tiers

## Margin And Support Burden

- current local-first architecture supports high gross margin
- lifetime pricing is simple but caps revenue per user
- monthly pricing could improve long-term revenue, but also raises churn and support expectations
- the current recommendation remains: prove paid demand first, then evaluate subscription expansion from real data
