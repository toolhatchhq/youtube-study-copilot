# Opportunity Brief

## User

- students using YouTube for courses, lectures, and revision
- language learners reviewing long educational videos
- self-learners who want notes and recall aids instead of passive watching

## Core Job

- turn a captioned YouTube video into notes, flashcards, quiz prompts, and exports without leaving the video page

## Why This Matters

- long videos are high-value but high-friction to review
- transcript-only tools solve speed, but not retention
- summary-only tools solve speed, but not active recall

## Demand Signals

- large existing Chrome Web Store demand for transcript and summary tools
- clear search intent around YouTube summary, transcript, notes, and study help
- adjacent traction for flashcard and spaced-repetition learning products

## Product Gap

- transcript tools are useful but shallow
- summary tools often optimize for quick consumption, not learning retention
- a study-first side panel with exports and saved packs is more specific and more defensible than generic summary alone

## Launch Thesis

- start with captioned YouTube videos only
- keep permissions and privacy posture simple
- win on “study output” rather than “AI summary”
